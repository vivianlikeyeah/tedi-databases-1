Getting Acquainted

What is a database?

Formally: Databases are an organised set of data.
Informally: An easy way to understand what a database is, is to consider a large microsoft excel sheet. You have a bunch of columns and a bunch of rows. You store information in an organised way. You can sort through data really quickly, and search really quickly.
To begin we’re going to jump in with a really superficial example.


Activity

Create a microsoft excel spreadsheet that stores the following information on everyone in your grade at TEDI London.
First Name Last Name Age Nationality Favourite Colour Dream Job Courses

Upload your excel spreadsheet

Now once you have your excel spreadsheet together, we’re going to use this as the foundation for learning about terminology that is essential to understand in order to work with databases.
This database has 7 fields. These are highlighted in orange. They are essentially the titles of each column. A piece of data, is essentially one cell. And all data has a data type. A2, A3 and A4 are strings. C2, C3 and C4 are integers. Each row in the database is known as a ‘record’. One example of a record is highlighted in purple.

There are 3 records in this database (Row 2, 3 and 4). Let’s assume we have 50 records (which you should have in your example). And we want to find all of the people who are over the age of 20. Let’s say we didn’t have our excel sheet. We would have to manually go through every record of every student and check. However, excel makes this process far more efficient. Add filters to each of your headings.

Click on ascending. Now your data is sorted and it’s much easier to identify all the students over the age of 20.
